# Charging Through Chaos: EV Market Intelligence in an Age of Global Instability

## Project Overview
This project explores the electric vehicle market through both a technical and strategic lens.

Using data from **103 EV models across 33 brands**, the analysis goes beyond standard product comparison to examine how pricing, range, efficiency, affordability, infrastructure, and geopolitical instability shape the future of EV adoption.

Rather than treating EVs as only a sustainability or engineering story, this project positions them within the realities of today’s world: supply chain disruptions, fragile mineral dependence, affordability barriers, infrastructure vulnerability, and consumer decision-making under uncertainty.

## Project Objective
The goal of this project was to analyze EV market dynamics and answer a broader strategic question:

**How does global instability affect EV users, markets, and long-term adoption, and what should analysts, businesses, and policymakers pay attention to?**

## Key Questions Explored
- How affordable are EVs across the market?
- Which brands and models offer the strongest efficiency and value?
- What market gaps exist across price tiers?
- How does global instability affect EV adoption and supply chains?
- What risks do EV users and manufacturers face in uncertain times?
- What practical recommendations can be made for key stakeholders?

## Dashboard Scope
- **103 EV models**
- **33 brands**
- **6 analytical dimensions**
- **6-page interactive dashboard**

## Dashboard Pages
1. **Executive Dashboard**  
   High-level overview of the EV market, major KPIs, and headline insights.

2. **Vehicle Explorer**  
   Interactive model-level exploration of EV specifications and market positioning.

3. **Price Analysis**  
   Analysis of affordability, pricing tiers, and consumer accessibility.

4. **Range & Efficiency**  
   Comparison of range, energy efficiency, and performance trade-offs.

5. **Conflict Impact**  
   Strategic interpretation of how geopolitics, supply chains, mineral concentration, and instability affect EV ecosystems.

6. **Advisory**  
   Recommendations for consumers, fleet operators, manufacturers, policymakers, and investors.

## Key Insights
- The average EV price remains high, creating affordability barriers for mass adoption.
- Only a small share of models fall into the lower-price segment, leaving emerging markets underserved.
- Efficiency is becoming a stronger competitive differentiator than top speed alone.
- EV growth is closely linked to global supply chain resilience and geopolitical stability.
- The future of EV adoption depends not only on innovation, but also on affordability, infrastructure, and strategic resilience.

## Business Value
This project demonstrates how data analysis can move beyond descriptive dashboards into **market intelligence and strategic storytelling**.

It combines technical EV data with business interpretation to help decision-makers think about:
- market accessibility
- product positioning
- geopolitical exposure
- resilience planning
- long-term adoption strategy
